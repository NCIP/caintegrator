Creating Kaplan-Meier Plots
The Kaplan Maier method is used for survival analysis of comparative groups of patients or samples. For example, you might identify a group of patients with smoking history and compare survival rates with a group of non-smoking patients. You could compare groups of patients with varying gene expression levels. The key is to first identify subsets of patients that meet criteria you want to establish, and calculate a Kaplan-Maier curves based on their survival probability as a function of time.
